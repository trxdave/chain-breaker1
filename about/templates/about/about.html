About Chain Breakers

Welcome to Chain Breakers! We are Hack to Hope, and this project was created as part of the "Code to Protect" hackathon in January 2025, hosted by Code Institute. This hackathon aligns with National Slavery and Human Trafficking Prevention Month, and our goal is to shine a light on the realities of human trafficking while empowering communities to recognize and respond to potential red flags.

At Chain Breakers, we aim to foster understanding, raise awareness, and equip individuals with tools to take meaningful action against human trafficking. By leveraging education and accessible resources, we believe in the power of knowledge to prevent exploitation and protect vulnerable individuals.

What We Do

Chain Breakers is a barrier-free platform dedicated to raising awareness about modern slavery and human trafficking. We prioritize accessibility and inclusivity, requiring no accounts or logins to access critical information. Our primary mission is to inform, educate, and empower individuals to recognize warning signs, take preventive action, and assist those in need.

Key Features

  • Realistic Scenarios: Engage with interactive, situation-based examples designed to highlight common red flags and forms of exploitation.
  • Educational Resources: Explore infographics, statistics, and practical tools to deepen your understanding of human trafficking.
  • Open Access: All content on Chain Breakers is free and publicly available—no sign-ups, no barriers.
  • Responsive Design: Access our resources seamlessly on any browser or device, anytime and anywhere.

Why This Matters

Human trafficking is a pervasive and hidden crisis affecting millions globally, including in the UK and EU. By providing actionable knowledge and accessible tools, we aim to break the cycle of exploitation and empower individuals to create a safer future.

Key Statistics

Global Statistics

  • 50 million people worldwide are trapped in modern slavery (Global Slavery Index, 2022).
  • Human trafficking generates $150 billion annually, with $99 billion coming from sexual exploitation (International Labour Organization, 2014).
  • 1 in 4 victims globally is a child (ILO, 2017).

UK & EU Statistics

  • In the UK, an estimated 136,000 people live in modern slavery (Global Slavery Index, 2022).
  • Over 14,000 victims are registered annually in the EU (European Commission, 2020).
  • 92% of EU trafficking victims are exploited for sexual or forced labor (European Commission, 2020).

To learn more about these statistics and their sources, visit our Statistics Page.

Our Mission

Our mission is simple: To provide accessible knowledge and tools that empower individuals and communities to take action against human trafficking. By removing barriers to information, Chain Breakers aspires to be a trusted resource for anyone committed to preventing modern slavery and supporting victims.

Let’s work together to break chains, restore freedom, and create a future where exploitation has no place.
